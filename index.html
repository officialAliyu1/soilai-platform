The Problem

Nigeria's oil & gas industry has left a legacy of degraded land

Decades of exploration and production have caused widespread environmental damage that current approaches fail to adequately address.

🛢️

Widespread Contamination

Oil & gas activities have caused soil degradation, hydrocarbon contamination, and loss of biological activity across Niger Delta host communities, affecting livelihoods and ecosystems.

📋

One-Size-Fits-All Approach

Current remediation uses uniform clean-up methods that ignore site-specific soil conditions — pH, organic carbon, contamination type — leading to poor long-term restoration outcomes.

🔄

Repeated Degradation Cycles

Without targeted restoration strategies, remediated lands often degrade again, trapping host communities in endless cycles of environmental damage and economic loss.

Our Solution

An integrated platform: soil science, sensors & AI

Three data layers feed a rule-based and AI-assisted analytical engine that generates precise, site-specific restoration recommendations.

LAYER 01

Field Soil Data

pH levels, electrical conductivity, organic carbon content, hydrocarbon contamination levels (TPH), heavy metal concentrations, and real-time soil moisture status from each unique site.

LAYER 02

Sensor & Lab Inputs

Low-cost field sensors and laboratory analysis provide real-time monitoring, validation data, and longitudinal tracking to ensure restoration is proceeding as modelled.

LAYER 03

Remote Sensing & GIS

Satellite imagery and geospatial data assess vegetation loss, land cover changes, and recovery patterns over time — enabling landscape-scale analysis and progress mapping.

AI-Assisted Analytical Engine

Rule-based and machine learning algorithms classify land degradation severity and generate prioritized, site-specific restoration recommendations — moving beyond guesswork to data-driven action plans that improve with every site assessed.

Restoration Pathways

Four site-specific restoration pathways

The platform matches each site to the most effective combination of evidence-based remediation strategies, selected based on actual soil and contamination data.

PATHWAY 01

Bioremediation & Phytoremediation

Microbial degradation strategies and targeted plant species selection based on contamination type and soil chemistry. The platform matches site conditions to proven bioremediation protocols and identifies the optimal plant species for hydrocarbon uptake and soil stabilisation.

Microbial treatment Plant selection TPH reduction
PATHWAY 02

Soil Amendment Recommendations

Tailored amendment prescriptions including biochar, compost, organic residues, and chemical stabilisers — selected based on soil deficiency analysis and local material availability. Targets pH correction, organic matter restoration, and structural improvement.

Biochar Compost pH correction
PATHWAY 03

Vegetation & Crop Recovery Species

Data-driven selection of vegetation and crop species best suited for ecosystem recovery on each specific site, considering soil chemistry, local climate, and community livelihood needs to ensure post-remediation land use is productive and sustainable.

Native species Food crops Ecosystem recovery
PATHWAY 04

Monitoring & Compliance Plans

Time-bound restoration schedules with milestone tracking, progress reporting, and automated compliance documentation for regulators and ESG reporting. Keeps operators accountable and provides verifiable proof of restoration progress.

Milestone tracking ESG reports Regulatory compliance
